Image as Commodity —

  1. Early Utility Patent from pre-existing technology
  2. Market for the mobile image, rising Bourgeois class
  3. Benjamin had already fled the Nazi Government and relocated to Paris

Viewer Relations —

  • Traditional aesthetic values of 'creativity and genius, eternal value and mystery — concepts whose uncontrolled application would lead to a processing of data in the Fascist sense' (p. 218)
  • He believed these values were generated by the bourgeois capitalist class, and amounted to what he called a 'theology of art' (p. 224)
  • Introduced concepts which were useless for the purposes of Fascism, but were useful for the formulation of 'revolutionary demands in the politics of art' (p. 218)
